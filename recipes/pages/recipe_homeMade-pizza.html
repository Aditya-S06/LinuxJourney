Homemade Pizza Recipe

There's nothing quite like a hot, fresh pizza made right in your own kitchen.
This homemade pizza recipe yields a crispy, chewy crust topped with your favorite
sauce and cheese. It's better than delivery and fun to make!

Ingredients:

  1. 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour or bread flour
  2. 1 teaspoon sugar
  3. 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  4. 1 teaspoon salt
  5. 1 cup warm water (110°F)
  6. 1 tablespoon olive oil
  7. 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  8. 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  9. Toppings of choice (pepperoni, vegetables, basil, etc.)

How to Make Homemade Pizza

Making pizza from scratch is easier than you think. Here is a brief overview of the process:
  1. Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let stand for 5-10 minutes until creamy.
  2. Mix flour, salt, and oil. Add the yeast mixture and knead until smooth and elastic.
  3. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until doubled.
  4. Punch down dough and stretch into a circle on a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  5. Top with sauce, cheese, and desired toppings.
  6. Bake in a preheated oven at 475°F (245°C) for 10-15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

The Best Flour for Pizza Dough

While all-purpose flour works well, using bread flour will give your crust a chewier texture and better structure. If you want an even crispier crust, you can try '00' flour, which is finely ground and traditional for Italian pizzas.

Baking Tips for the Perfect Crust

For the best results, preheat your oven as high as it will go (usually 475°F-500°F). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats. The high heat is crucial for getting that pizzeria-style crust—crispy on the outside and airy on the inside.
